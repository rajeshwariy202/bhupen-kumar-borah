What Drives Bhupen Kumar Borah

The future of Assam, a northeastern state of India, will be shaped by a combination of economic growth, environmental challenges, social dynamics, and political developments. Key economic drivers include agriculture, tea production, and tourism, all of which hold significant potential if supported by sustainable practices and better infrastructure.

Enhanced connectivity through road, rail, and air networks can further boost trade and investment. Meanwhile, the state's rich biodiversity and cultural heritage position it well for tourism-led development. Addressing environmental issues, such as flooding and deforestation, will be essential to ensuring long-term sustainability.
